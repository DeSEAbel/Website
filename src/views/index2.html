DeSEAbel Project

Founded in 2022, DeSEAbel is a non-profit association dedicated to tackling maritime noise pollution. Born out of the Ocean Hackathon, our team is passionate about reducing this environmental threat. Noise pollution is a pervasive issue, disrupting marine ecosystems and impacting human health. Our current work is based on three main missions.

Raise awareness

We aim to raise awareness about the impact of maritime noise on the ocean environment.

Open source App

We are developing an open-source simulation tool with robust mathematical models to visualize the impact of noise pollution on the ocean.

Ocean Data

We are aggregating global data to provide comprehensive insights into noise pollution's effects on the ocean.

Noise Pollution

Since 2005, the United Nations has acknowledged underwater noise from human activities as a form of marine pollution and a dire threat to marine ecosystems. Despite France recognizing underwater noise as pollution since 2010, no concrete restrictions have been enforced.

The ocean's acoustic environment is a symphony of natural and human-made sounds, including seismic events, animal communications, and anthropogenic activities like maritime traffic and offshore operations. Communication is vital for marine life survival, especially for species like marine mammals, which rely on diverse frequencies for navigation, feeding, mating and group cohesion. However, human intervention has disrupted this delicate balance. Maritime traffic has skyrocketed, doubling from 1950 to 2000, adding three decibels to ocean sound levels every decade.

This incessant noise poses grave threats to marine life, resulting in hearing loss, heightened stress, impaired feeding, reproductive difficulties and catastrophic die-offs near major noise sources.

Reducing Ocean Noise Pollution

Ship Recommendations
  • Reduction of ship speed
  • sensitive areas if possible
  • Ship design and diesel-electric propulsion: hybrid (avoid vibrations)
  • Smooth hull and innovative propellers
  • Ship maintenance
Pile Driving Recommendations
  • Adapt activities according to the biological cycle of species
  • Bubble curtains
