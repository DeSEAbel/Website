About

DeSEAbel is a free and open source application developed to adapt human activities to reduce the volume of noise in the oceans.

The application is intended to be continually improved by a community of motivated individuals who share a common interest in ecology.

With this tool, we aim to raise awareness of the impact of noise in the ocean while providing a decision support tool to limit the ecological impact of various activities, whether on an individual, corporate or governmental scale.

Our motivations

Since 2005, the United Nations has recognized underwater noise from human activities as a source of marine pollution and a threat to marine ecosystems. In France, underwater noise has been recognized as a source of pollution since 2010, but no restrictions have yet been put in place. In the oceans, there is a diversity of sounds related to physical phenomena (earthquakes, volcanic activity, waves, ice melting), fauna communication and anthropic activities (maritime traffic, offshore activities, sonar, port activities). Communication is an essential element for marine fauna to survive and reproduce in the ocean and for a part of the species and especially the marine mammals of the beaches. especially the marine mammals, with a wide range of frequencies to communicate, to orient themselves, to communicate, to orient themselves, to find their food, to avoid predators, to detect predators, to detect mates, to locate rivals or to maintain the cohesion of the group. or to maintain the cohesion of the group.

Humans have significantly altered the soundscape of the oceans. Maritime traffic roughly doubled between 1950 and 2000. Vessel traffic adds 3 decibels to the sound level of the oceans every 10 years, posing a major threat to aquatic life. Numerous studies have shown a variety of effects on marine species associated with this noise pollution: hearing loss, increased stress, loss of feeding ability, difficulty reproducing or responding to predators, and even mass die-offs of species in front of a major noise source.

Reducing Ocean Noise Pollution: Suggestions

Ship Recommendations:

  • Reduction of ship speed
  • Avoid sensitive areas if possible
  • Ship design and diesel-electric propulsion: hybrid (avoid vibrations)
  • Smooth hull and innovative propellers
  • Ship maintenance

Pile Driving Recommendations:

  • Adapt activities according to the biological cycle of species
  • Bubble curtains
